# The Problem

Traditional engineering systems capture visible output:

* GitHub captures commits and pull requests.
* Jira captures tickets and completion status.
* IDE analytics capture activity and usage.
* Performance systems capture manager feedback and outcomes.

These systems do not capture the cognitive work behind the output.

They cannot reliably answer questions such as:

* Who identified the architectural flaw before implementation?
* Who corrected the AI when it made an unsafe assumption?
* Who recognized the hidden system constraint?
* Who introduced the product insight that changed the direction?
* Which tasks required genuine engineering judgment?
* Where did the developer lose time fighting tooling or configuration?
* Which valuable ideas appeared during execution but were never pursued?

As AI produces more of the visible implementation, these questions become increasingly important.

Without a record of the reasoning process, a developer's true contribution risks becoming invisible.
